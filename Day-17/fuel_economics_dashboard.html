Executive Summary

Across 52 real-world usage records spanning five fuel/powertrain categories — CNG, Diesel, Electric (EV), Petrol (E20), and E85 Flex-Fuel — Electric (EV) emerges as the clear economic winner, with the lowest combined cost per kilometer (₹1.96/km) and the lowest estimated 5-year ownership running cost (≈ ₹1,17,800) at an assumed usage of 12,000 km/year. CNG is the most economical among combustion-based options (₹3.97/km), while Petrol (E20) and E85 Flex-Fuel are the most expensive to run (₹6.6–6.8/km) despite E85's relatively lower price per liter, largely due to poor fuel efficiency (~13 km/l average in this dataset). On emissions, E85 Flex-Fuel unexpectedly shows the lowest average CO₂ output per km (0.069 kg/km) in this dataset — even lower than EV — while Diesel is the highest emitter (0.177 kg/km). This dashboard quantifies fuel cost, maintenance cost, annual running cost, 5-year ownership cost, cost per km, and carbon emissions for each fuel type, and identifies the most economical and lowest-carbon options based on the uploaded dataset.
